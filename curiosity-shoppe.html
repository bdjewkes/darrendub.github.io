Curiosity Shoppe Icons

Summary

Inkling began by building interactive, digital textbooks. However, over the years, Inkling expanded its library to include travel guides, medical references, and cookbooks to mobile devices near you. They needed a visual system to bring these new categories under one roof.

Results

I created twelve unique icons to represent our categories. These icons help strengthen the connection between categories and interactive inkling eBooks and communicated that Inkling has relevant content for every user. They were well-received and began to make their into the product, website, and marketing materials.

The Process

In its early days, Inkling leveraged illustrations to help bring its categories to life. The illustrations were beautiful, smart, human, and inspiring. This is an example of the illustrations used to help onboard new users into the product:

Designed by Kien Nguyen

Inspiration

Our art director at the time, Brendan, had begun collect inspiration and a designer that really stood out was Noma Bar. His work entices viewers to survey the entire piece and really study it until the arrangement of colors and space would reveal the hidden message. This would be especially important later in the design process.

Concepts

We identified a handful of concepts we wanted to emulate in these illustrations:

  • Delightful
  • Curious
  • Deeper Meaning
  • Narrative
  • Lets the viewer connect the dots

History Lesson

The Inkling logo was based on this idea of a slow reveal, unveiling the full work in front of the viewers eyes. I was interested in this for a few reasons.

  • This style and conception was deeply tied to our company's history
  • The gaps within these icons made the whole composition more interesting. Causing a viewer to look a little more closely.
  • These icons empowered users to try to piece together what they were looking at and they had an ah-ha moment once they got it.

New Beginnings

Just after this launch, Inkling began to pivot to a B2B market. Without our store and category offering these icons did not make much sense in our new B2B world. However, the core styles and ideas developed during this process would become the foundation in the illustration and iconography we create today.
